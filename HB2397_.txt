TWENTY-SIXTH LEGISLATURE, 2012
 
STATE OF HAWAII
 
 
 
 
 
 
A BILL FOR AN ACT
 
 
RELATING TO CAPITAL IMPROVEMENT PROJECTS FOR THE BENEFIT OF THE SIXTH REPRESENTATIVE DISTRICT.
 
 
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
 

SECTION 1.  The following sums, or so much thereof as shall be sufficient to finance the projects listed in this Act, are hereby appropriated out of the general revenues of the State of Hawaii for fiscal year 2012-2013:
   1.  To be expended by the natural energy laboratory of Hawaii authority:
    A.  Queen Kaahumanu highway, Hawaii
        Design, environmental assessment, and construction of a frontage road to accommodate closure of entrance due to widening of Queen Kaahumanu highway.
        Design and environmental assessment       $500,000
        Construction                            $6,500,000
        Total funding                           $7,000,000
    2.  To be expended by the department of defense:
A. Kona airport, Hawaii
        Civil air patrol. Renovation and expansion                  $665,000
          Total funding                             $665,000
    B.  Veteran's center, Hawaii
        Site selection and planning of a veteran's center, west Hawaii.
        Site selection and planning               $100,000
        Total funding                             $100,000
    3.  To be expended by the department of land and natural resources:
    A.  Honokohau marina and small boat harbor, Hawaii
          Design and construction of parking and drainage improvements.
          Design and construction                   $650,000
        Total funding                             $650,000
    4. To be expended by the department of health:
      A.  Lai Opua clinic, Hawaii
        Construction of a clinic.
        Construction                            $1,700,000
  Total funding                           $1,700,000
SECTION 2.  The sums appropriated for the respective capital projects set forth in section 1 shall be expended by the designated expending agency for the purposes of this Act.
SECTION 3.  This Act shall take effect on July 1, 2012.
