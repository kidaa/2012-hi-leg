STATE OF HAWAII
 
 
 
 
 

HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
 
 
ENCOURAGING THE GOVERNOR TO TRANSFER THE RESPONSIBILITIES FOR THE ADMINISTRATION OF THE OFFICE OF INTERNATIONAL AFFAIRS FROM THE DEPARTMENT OF BUSINESS, ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT AND TOURISM TO THE LIEUTENANT GOVERNOR.
 
 

     WHEREAS, Hawaii plays a critical role in international affairs, given its central location in the Pacific Ocean; and
 
     WHEREAS, in November 2011, Hawaii hosted the 23rd Annual Leader's Meeting of the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC); and
 
     WHEREAS, the success of this international summit was greatly influenced by efforts of the APEC Hawaii Host Committee, which was comprised of important government, business and educational leaders; and
 
     WHEREAS, the success of APEC demonstrated that Hawaii is a credible, convenient and legitimate location to conduct international business and affairs; and
 
WHEREAS, the State's Lieutenant Governor served on the APEC Hawaii Host Committee and played a critical role in coordinating government resources; and
 
     WHEREAS, the Lieutenant Governor's key role demonstrates the importance of including international outreach in the scope of the Executive Branch responsibilities; now, therefore,
 
     BE IT RESOLVED by the House of Representatives of the Twenty-sixth Legislature of the State of Hawaii, Regular Session of 2012, the Senate concurring, that the Governor of the State of Hawaii transfer the responsibilities of administering the Office of International Affairs from the Department of Business, Economic Development and Tourism to the Office of the Lieutenant Governor; and
 
     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that certified copies of this Concurrent Resolution be transmitted to the Governor of the State of Hawaii, the Lieutenant Governor of the State of Hawaii, and the Director of the Department of Business, Economic Development and Tourism.
