STATE OF HAWAII
 
 
 
 
 
 
A BILL FOR AN ACT
 
 
RELATING TO VEHICLE AUDIO EQUIPMENT.
 
 
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
 

     SECTION 1.  Chapter 291, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is amended by adding a new section to be appropriately designated and to read as follows:
     "§291-    Aftermarket motor vehicle audio equipment; excessive sound amplification prohibited.  (a)  No person shall install, possess, or operate in any motor vehicle an in-dash or mounted radio, cassette tape, compact disc, digital video disc, or digital music device other than the original equipment in which there are:
     (1)  Five or more speakers, including subwoofers or any other speakers not installed in the passenger compartment;
     (2)  Any speaker exceeding one hundred watts power output;
     (3)  Any speaker exceeding six and one-half inches in width or height; or
     (4)  Any speaker installed external to the passenger compartment or in an open hatch back.
This section shall not apply to original manufacturer equipment installed in the vehicle by the manufacturer or dealer.
(b)  Any violation of this section shall constitute a violation under section 701-107(5) and shall be enforceable by police officers.  The fine for this violation shall be not less than $25 nor more than $250 for each separate offense.  Any person who violates this section may be issued a summons or citation for such violation.
(c)  As used in this section, "subwoofer" means a complete loudspeaker dedicated to the reproduction of low-pitched audio frequencies."
     SECTION 2.  This Act does not affect rights and duties that matured, penalties that were incurred, and proceedings that were begun, before its effective date.
     SECTION 3.  New statutory material is underscored.
     SECTION 4.  This Act shall take effect upon its approval.
