TWENTY-SIXTH LEGISLATURE, 2011
 
STATE OF HAWAII
 
 
 
 
 
 
A BILL FOR AN ACT
 
 
RELATING TO POWER OF ATTORNEY.
 
 
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
 

     SECTION 1.  Chapter 551D, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is amended by adding a new section to be appropriately designated and to read as follows:
     "§551D-     Notice of designation and subsequent acts.  A designated attorney-in-fact who is a child of the principal shall provide:
     (1)  A written copy of the executed power of attorney; and
     (2)  Notice of any acts taken pursuant to the power of attorney;
to any other child of the principal who is not designated as an attorney-in-fact of the principal."
     SECTION 2.  New statutory material is underscored.
     SECTION 3.  This Act shall take effect upon its approval.

Report Title:
Durable Power of Attorney
 
Description:
Requires a designated attorney-in-fact who is a child of the principal to give notice of the designation and any subsequent acts to any child of the principal who is not designated as attorney-in-fact.
