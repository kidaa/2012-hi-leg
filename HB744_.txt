HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
H.B. NO.
744
TWENTY-SIXTH LEGISLATURE, 2011
 
STATE OF HAWAII
 
 
 
 
 
 
A BILL FOR AN ACT
 
 
RELATING TO DAM SAFETY.
 
 
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
 

     SECTION 1.  (a)  There is established the dam safety task force, to be placed within the department of land and natural resources for administrative purposes only.
     (b)  The dam safety task force shall consist of the following members:
     (1)  The chairperson of the board of land and natural resources or the chairperson's designee, who shall serve as the chairperson of the task force;
     (2)  The chairperson of the board of agriculture or the chairperson's designee;
     (3)  The attorney general or the attorney general's designee;
     (4)  The deputy director for the commission on water resource management of the department of land and natural resources or the deputy director's designee;
     (5)  A member of the Hawaii Cattlemen's Council, who shall be requested to participate;
     (6)  A representative of the Hawaii Farm Bureau Federation, who shall be requested to participate;
     (7)  An owner of a parcel of agricultural land in the State greater than     acres that contains at least one dam, to be selected by the chairperson, who shall be requested to participate; and
     (8)  Five persons with specialized knowledge and experience in water resource management and engineering, to be selected by the chairperson, who shall be requested to participate.
     (c)  The dam safety task force shall review applicable state laws, administrative rules, and county ordinances and recommend actions to be taken by the engineering division of the department of land and natural resources in its administration of the Hawaii dam safety program.
     (d)  The members of the dam safety task force shall serve without compensation but shall be reimbursed for expenses, including travel expenses, necessary for the performance of their duties.
     (e)  The dam safety task force shall report its findings and recommendations, including any proposed legislation, to the legislature no later than twenty days prior to the convening of the regular session of 2012.
     (f)  The dam safety task force shall terminate on June 30, 2012.
     SECTION 2.  This Act shall take effect upon its approval.

Report Title:
Dam Safety; Dam Safety Task Force
 
Description:
